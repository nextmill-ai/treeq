## Notes for TreeQ's own agreement (working observations)

These are NOT part of the PHC Rx T&Cs — captured here as drafting cues for TreeQ's own software agreement:

1. **Decision-aid framing.** PHC Rx leans heavily on "diagnostic aid, NOT an absolute decision maker." TreeQ should similarly position the cut-cost estimate as an *estimate / decision-support tool*, not a binding quote or appraisal.
2. **User-qualification clause.** PHC Rx requires users to be "qualified professionals." TreeQ may want a parallel "you are an arborist / tree-care operator, or estimating on behalf of one" representation.
3. **Indemnification is broad and survives termination.** Worth mirroring, especially for damages arising from estimates TreeQ produces that a customer relies on.
4. **Liability carve-outs to mirror:** loss of profits, property damage, personal injury, regulatory fines, and "any other damages arising from use or inability to use the application." The "plant damage, death, or decline" line is PHC-specific; TreeQ's analog is **inaccurate cut-cost estimates, missed bids, or under/overpricing**.
5. **"Product label is the law" analog.** TreeQ has no pesticide labels, but the equivalent grounding is **ANSI A300 standards, OSHA 1910.269 / ANSI Z133 safety rules, and local permit requirements** — users remain responsible regardless of what TreeQ outputs.
6. **Governing law.** PHC Rx → Texas. TreeQ should default to **Delaware** (TreeQ Inc. C-corp domicile per corporate structure plan) or **New York** (current operations / Rochester market) — pick one and be consistent.
7. **AS IS / AS AVAILABLE language.** Standard SaaS boilerplate; TreeQ should adopt verbatim with its entity name swapped in.
8. **Modification clause.** PHC Rx allows unilateral changes with continued-use = acceptance. Standard, but for paid TreeQ subscribers consider a 30-day notice requirement for *material* changes.
9. **What PHC Rx is missing that TreeQ should add:**
   - Subscription / payment / refund terms (PHC Rx mentions a free trial in UI but the T&Cs don't cover billing)
   - Data ownership / customer-data clause (who owns property photos, measurements, customer addresses, bid history)
   - Privacy Policy reference + link
   - Account termination / suspension grounds
   - Export of customer data on cancellation
   - Acceptable Use / no-reverse-engineering / no-scraping clause
   - DMCA / IP-infringement notice address
   - Force majeure
   - Severability + entire-agreement clause
   - Arbitration vs. court (PHC Rx defaults to courts; many SaaS T&Cs use AAA arbitration with a class-action waiver)
   - Age / capacity to contract (18+, authority to bind employer)
